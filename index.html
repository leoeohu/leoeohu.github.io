About

A high-schooler who likes to make.

I'm a high school student fascinated by how things work — from the shaders that paint a virtual rainstorm, to the gates inside an FPGA, to the reactions on a lab bench. I like projects that sit at the seams between disciplines.

Most of what I build starts as curiosity and turns into a tool someone can actually use. The pieces below are three of my favorites: a cinematic 3D scene, an accessibility tool for blind learners, and an AI-driven texture generator.

Accessibility · Open tool

Tactile Learning Tool

For blind learners · Access on GitHub

Turns an idea or a reference image into a 3D-printable tactile relief plate for blind students — plus standard-spaced Braille / 盲文 labels. AI generates a clean line drawing, a height map becomes an embossed solid, and Blender exports a watertight, flat-bottomed STL that prints support-free.

AI Texture Generator

Blender add-on · Preview only

A small tool that turns a text prompt into a seamless, tileable PBR-ready texture and drops it straight onto a material — copper, marble, brick, woven linen, anything you can describe. Below are four samples it generated. This one's a peek behind the curtain rather than a download.

$ gen_texture.py "polished carrara marble, seamless tileable" → marble.png
